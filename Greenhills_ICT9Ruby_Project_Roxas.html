A fire can start anywhere, so it's critical to always be prepared for what to do if one breaks out inside your house. It is equally crucial to consider all of the ways in which you can stop one from occurring in the first place. To safeguard your home and the people you care about, you need to learn how to put out a fire. Of course, you can purchase more straightforward fire safety measures like smoke alarms, fire extinguishers, an escape plan, and even fireproof safe deposit boxes.

However, the truth is that there are some very easy things you can do to make yourself safer.


How to Prevent Fires

Unplug not in use electronics		 Having alot of energy and electricity flowing through your house all at once can be dangerouse, and could lead to being a fire hazzard. It is important to organize your wires, and to use less energy as it not only could save the planet, but also save your life.

De-Clutter		 In case of a fire, you should de-clutter because it could cause a spontaneous fire and the clutter could fuel it more, it is also important to organize one self and keep space, so that in case of pottential fire, it will spread slower.

Check Wires and Electricals		 It is important to keep on checking electric wires, in case of them being frayed or damaged, and find the problem and try to fix it, or replace it, as it could cause fires, especially near a large clutter of wires and other electronics. You want to keep the stack of papers away from your wax melter. You also want to make sure that you’re watching closely for anything that could turn into a problem. You don’t want a fire to pop up anywhere and that means watching heat sources and anything close to them.

Don’t smoke in your house		 If you do smoke, you should do it in a well-ventilated area and remember to completely put out the cigarette before you throw it away. Cigarettes have a variety of materials inside of them that can continue to burn, even if you think that you’ve put them outx.
